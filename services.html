General Contracting Process

The client appoints an architect to design the project and to prepare the necessary drawings and specifications.

The architect in consultation with client completes the drawings and issues them for pricing.

The client selects the general contractor and project is awarded.

CTV manage the subcontractors and documentation while supervising and controlling construction.

Execution of project is based purely on defined Budget, Delivery schedule & Scope parameters.

Costing, Bidding & Contract type

As the general contractor, CTV goes by Design - Bid - Built process and provides a confirmed price after design is complete and before construction begins. An estimate is done based on the cost of materials, equipment and labor.

Unit Price Method

Client pays according to the quantity of material used with unit prices fixed in advance.
Square Basis Method

Client pays for per square feet of built up area.
Lump Sum Method

Client pays a fixed price for the project.

Interior Design Process

Concept Creation

The first part of the Construction design process at CTV always starts with a concept. It is here that we create an agreed upon flow to your space.

The concepts are evolved and designed through Interactive, Collaborative & Creative sessions with the clients.

Operating System

After a concept is established, the project's team develops the scope of your space further by developing the home's design features and selecting material finishes, plumbing fixtures, lighting, furniture and accessories.

This is also the phase where we work closely with an architect to develop your construction Documents, including all Plumbing and Electrical plans.

Construction

Interior construction CTV has its own staff of estimators, project managers, superintendents, and senior management for interior construction. The entire staff is a dedicated group of professionals experienced in the particular demands of an upfit, renovation and expansion.

Project Management

At CTV, every project is assigned a dedicated Project Manager (PM). The PM is responsible for ensuring a flawless and timely execution of construction.

We work very closely with all our vendors, trades and sub - contractors during the project's partnership. Your PM is responsible for your project's finances and purchasing.

Project Turnover

A very exciting and emotional day to look forward to your perfectly staged new home!

This is a crucial turning point in the project as every last detail is solidified.

During this time we all pull together and take on certain tasks: shopping for accessories, wrapping up any last minute touch ups, moving in all of your brand new furniture with great care, cleaning and hanging every last piece of wall art.
